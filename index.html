Why Not Call it What it is?

Growing up, I absolutely *lived* for my grandmothers cooking. My inner and outer fat kids would rejoice at the meals she prepared. But she was human, just like the rest of us. Sometimes things didn't always come out the way she'd planned. Sometimes she didn't always have all the ingredients on hand. So if things didn't work out quite right, she would just give it a different name. It was her way of not just being merciful with herself, but with the world around her; it was her way of accepting that we all have flaws, and that's not always a bad thing. The food she made might not have been perfect to whomever wrote the recipe, but it was perfect to us, so we were always happy to call it something else.

I've taken the idea of mercy with oneself and that same almost childlike wisdom and incorporated it to the design choices herein.
